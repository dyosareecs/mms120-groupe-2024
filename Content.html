Understanding Disruptive Technologies

Technology has evolved to the point where digital products and services now offer new ways to enhance and improve work across various industries. These innovations are called "Disruptive Technologies" and the term was introduced by Clayton Christensen and Joseph Bower (Schuelke-Leech, 2017).

Such digital products are altering new methods of how consumers, industries, or businesses operate. These innovations sweep away established workflows and processes (Smith, 2022), which is why they’re considered disruptive.

  • Voice Assistants (Alexa, Siri, Google Assistant)

    These technologies transformed how individuals interact with digital systems. Voice assistants create new touchpoints for customer interactions, streamlining processes and reshaping industries like retail, marketing, and customer service.

  • Electric Vehicles

    EVs or Electric Vehicles pose challenges to traditional combustion engine vehicles, especially with the growing concerns over climate change and CO2 emissions.

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI)

    AIs are known to automate jobs, which help consumers skip time-consuming processes giving us more efficient results in complex tasks, most especially in education and healthcare industries.

  • E-Commerce Apps (Shopee, Lazada)

    These applications are shifting shopping from physical stores to online platforms, reducing the need for in-store visits.

  • Smartwatches (Apple/Samsung Watch)

    Smartwatches revolutionized how we associate with technology and manage personal health. By integrating health tracking, such as heart rate and sleep patterns, they challenge traditional fitness tools. They also offer new convenience by allowing users to receive notifications, make payments, and control apps directly from their wrist.

TRADITIONAL IN-PERSON DATING

Dating in person in the Philippines involves lots of nuances which stems from the old traditional dating cultures, particularly the custom of ligaw or courtship. Unlike in Western cultures, where the direct question “Will you be my girlfriend?” is common, in the Philippines, the process begins with the more formal and respectful “Can I court you?” This question signifies the suitor’s intention to prove himself worthy of the woman’s affection while respecting her autonomy in the decision.

Key Elements of Traditional Courtship

Ligaw (Courtship) Pagdalaw (Regular Visits) Harana (Serenade)

This is a period where the suitor demonstrates his sincerity and commitment through consistent effort, patience, and gestures of respect. For many, ligaw remains a cherished aspect of Filipino relationships today.

Consistently visiting the woman’s home allowed the suitor to build a connection with her and her family, reflecting the importance of familial approval in relationships.

A traditional way of professing love, a suitor would sing romantic songs outside the woman’s house, often accompanied by live music. This act symbolized both romantic interest and dedication.

CHALLENGES OF TRADITIONAL DATING

  • Societal Pressure and expectations

    Traditional dating involves a lot of factors, including pressures, expectations, and influences from family and the community. There are pressures to conform to societal expectations, such as dating for marriage, gender roles, and certain rituals and norms.

  • Limited Opportunities

    People are restricted when it comes to traditional dating, as it relies on personal connections, chance encounters, and social groups such as school, the workplace, or neighborhood, which limits options and exposure to diverse people and backgrounds.

  • Time-consuming

    Going on in-person dates, setting up dates, and attending various social events with the hopes of meeting someone new can take up a lot of time. Relationships also develop more slowly in traditional dating because of cultural norms, such as gradual courtship.

  • Financially Consuming

    Traditional in-person dating can be financially consuming, especially when going on multiple dates with the hopes of finding the right person. Dinner at a restaurant, transportation, entertainment, and gifts can become overwhelming. In traditional dating, there are unequal financial expectations due to gender roles and norms that expect that one partner, traditionally the man, to pay for most or all expenses.

  • Limited Access to Information

    Compared to modern dating, which uses dating apps that include profiles and backgrounds, traditional dating is a blank slate in which the initial impressions may be based on physical appearance or the interactions themselves when they meet face-to-face.
